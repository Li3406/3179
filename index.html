Domestic Tourism in Malaysia, 2023

Explore the evolving trends of domestic tourism in Malaysia through this interactive dashboard, offering insights into travel patterns, popular destinations, and key factors shaping local tourism.

Visitor Distribution Across States

Selangor and Kuala Lumpur attracted a high volume of visitors, likely due to their strong economies and urban growth, while more rural states like Perlis and Kelantan saw fewer tourists.

Trends in Visitors

Tourism faced a major setback during the coronavirus disease pandemic, with visitor numbers dropping sharply. Yet by 2022, domestic tourism began a swift recovery.

Visitor-Driven Expenditure

Visitor numbers and spending moved in tandem. As tourism fell during the pandemic, so did expenditure. When visitors returned, their spending surged, underscoring tourism's role in economic recovery.

Where Visitors Spent the Most Cash

Visitors focus their spending primarily on tangible goods and services, with shopping as the top category, followed by food and beverages.

Visitors' Transport Preferences

Private vehicles were the dominant mode of transportation for domestic visitors in Malaysia.

This preference helps explain why spending on automotive fuel ranked third, tying with accommodation in visitor expenditures.
