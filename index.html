Domestic Tourism in Malaysia

Domestic tourism plays a crucial role in sustaining local economies, fostering cultural exchange, and boosting employment opportunities. Especially during the COVID-19 pandemic, the focus on internal tourism becomes even more important as communities rely on local travelers to support businesses and maintain economic stability.

Visitor Distribution Across Malaysian States

States such as Selangor and Kuala Lumpur attract a large share of visitors, likely due to their economic significance, urban development, and accessibility. Meanwhile, states like Perlis and Kelantan experience lower visitor numbers, possibly due to their more rural and less industrialized nature.

Trends in Domestic Tourism (2016-2023)

Domestic tourism experienced a significant rise in visitors in 2023 after struggling for a few years due to the COVID-19 pandemic. In 2021, the number of visitors plummeted by 72.4% compared to 2019, marking the sharpest decline and the lowest figures recorded. However, starting in 2022, the data reveals a strong recovery.
